    Telescope field of view
    
          
    
            Diameter in degrees of the largest circle that fits entirely inside your image
            (the inscribed circle on the sensor). For a rectangular chip this is the circle that touches the
            short sides — on a square sensor (e.g. IMX533) it equals the short-side field width.
            ToO mosaics offset each pointing by this amount; tile centers on an n×n grid are
            (n−1)×FOV apart (e.g. 8×8 → ~7×FOV between outer centers).
            A single pointing is used when the alert error radius is ≤ FOV/2 (localization fits
            in one inscribed circle); larger errors tile with n = ceil(2×err / FOV) (max 8×8).

    Sky Watchdog
    
          
    
            While a sequence is running the watchdog checks OCS every 60 s.
            If conditions go bad the mount is parked automatically.
            After Retention minutes of clear conditions it unparks and re-slews.
            If conditions stay bad longer than Max bad duration (e.g. dawn), the sequence is aborted.
